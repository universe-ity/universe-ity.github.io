        "## 3. Find the curvature of each surface\n",
        "Using the Lens Maker's formula at the beginning of this section, the radius of curvature $r$ for a bi-convex lens, assuming both surfaces have the same curvature ($r=|r_1|=|r_2|$), is \n",
        "\n",
        "$$r = \\frac{f}{2(n-1)}$$ \n",
        "\n",
        "<div class=\"alert alert-info\">\n",
        "\n",
        "Note\n",
        "\n",
        "You should verify this result!\n",
        "\n",
        "</div>\n",
        "\n",
        "OSLO defines positive radii as those with a center to the right of the vertex:\n",
        "\n",
        "![https://omlc.org/classroom/oslotut/simple/curvature1.png](https://omlc.org/classroom/oslotut/simple/curvature1.png)\n",
        "\n",
        "OSLO defines negative radii as those with a center to the left of the vertex:\n",
        "\n",
        "![https://omlc.org/classroom/oslotut/simple/curvature2.png](https://omlc.org/classroom/oslotut/simple/curvature2.png)\n",
        "\n",
        "**IMPORTANT:** The object and image surfaces also have radii of curvatures. Since they are flat, their curvatures are that of a circle that is infinitely large and thus their curvatures are infinite. OSLO allows one to write 0 to represent infinity for radius of curvature.\n",
